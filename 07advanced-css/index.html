History of Gamer's Santuary

Video game enthusiast and founder of Gamer's Sanctuary, Wyatt Barlow believes that everyone should have an equal oppurtunity to enjoy video and board games "without having to break" your bank account. His very first video game console was a N64 which came with his all time favorite game, The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time. Today, games of this generation can run you to the bank begging for a loan, that is why he created Gamer's Santuary so that new and returning players can experience the love that he has for video/board games at an affordable price.

Reason's for price mark ups

  • Research shows that the upcoming consoles from Sony and Microsoft look much more historically competitive once inflation is taken into account (and once expected price drops are extrapolated).
  • In 1982, a copy of Activision's Tennis would run you $19.99 in Sears' holiday wishbook. That may sound like a bargain basement price, but it's actually equivalent to a moderate $48.18 in 2013 dollars.
  • As 1996 came around, prices started to fall back to historic norms somewhat, as retailers cleared out excess stock of increasingly outdated 16-bit games and got rid of cartridges for failed systems like the 32X and Jaguar.
